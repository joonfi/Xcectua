# Governance Change Proposal (GCP)

## Purpose
A Governance Change Proposal (GCP) is the **mandatory entry point**
for any governance‑impacting change.

No informal governance evolution is allowed.

---

## Required Sections

### 1. Summary
What is changing and why.

### 2. Motivation
Problem statement and justification.

### 3. Scope
Files, behaviors, or processes affected.

### 4. Phase‑2 Impact
Explicit confirmation that Phase‑2 invariants are not weakened.

### 5. Diff Classification
Type‑0 / Type‑1 / Type‑2 / Type‑3 (per classification doc).

### 6. Evidence Requirements
What evidence is required and why.

### 7. Risk Assessment
Operational and governance risks.

---

## Rules

- Every governance change requires a GCP.
- GCPs are reviewed via PR.
- Evidence must accompany the PR.
- Approval does not bypass `rexce/validate`.

---

## Authority

GCPs document **intent**.
Only `rexce` determines **validity**.
